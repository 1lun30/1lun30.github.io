EXPLORATION
Financial Crisis

The financial crisis of 2007–2008, also known as the global financial crisis (GFC), was a severe worldwide financial crisis. The global youth unemployment rate, from the outbreak of the financial crisis in 2008 to 2009, increased from 11.9% to 12.8%. In just one year, it added 5 million people. In 2011, protests led by youths took place in more than 1,000 cities in 82 countries around the world, calling for work and social justice.

Taiwan's Influence
Attenuation of consumption

The youth unemployment crisis is changing the dynamics of families, as parents need to continue to support their grown-up children, and unemployed young people delay starting a family, making it more frugal. The Government's efforts to provide unemployment benefits, in which a large number of young people would have become consumers, have suffered from unemployment and, most importantly, youth unemployment is disintegrating young people's belief in education, which has been believed in in the past as the way to a richer life, only to find that their qualifications are not so effective in the bleak job market.
